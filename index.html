How this works: Browsers restrict raw network access for security. This tool uses WebRTC to discover your local IP, then probes common ports via timed fetch requests. Results show devices that respond to HTTP/HTTPS. For full network scanning, use tools like nmap or Angry IP Scanner locally.

🔒 Permission Required

To scan your local network, NetScan needs your consent. Here's exactly what it will do:

Detect your local IP via WebRTC
Probe IPs in your subnet (no data sent externally)
Check common HTTP ports (80, 443, 8080…)
All processing stays in your browser
